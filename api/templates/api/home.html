Farm technology

From the poultry shed to your screen

The system is designed to convert farm activity into useful information. Sensor readings and operational records are organized into one dashboard so management can respond faster and maintain a reliable history.

01

Collect accurate information

Sensors and daily entries capture environmental, mortality, feed, medicine, sales and expense data.

02

Organize records automatically

Information is linked to the correct batch, date, investor allocation and financial report.

03

Identify risks earlier

AI-assisted rules can highlight temperature, humidity, mortality or cost patterns that require attention.

04

Support better decisions

Management receives clearer information for ventilation, feeding, sales timing and profitability planning.

How the system works

A complete information flow from farm to management

Every record becomes part of a connected process that improves visibility, control and accountability.

1

Farm Activity

Birds are received, feed is issued, medicine is used, mortality is recorded and sales take place.

2

Digital Recording

Each activity is entered against the correct batch to build an accurate operational history.

3

Monitoring and Analysis

The platform combines records with sensor readings to show trends, exceptions and financial results.

4

Management Action

Authorized users review dashboards and take informed action based on current farm conditions.

Management platform

Operational control with financial transparency

RayNoor’s management system is built to connect flock performance with cost and revenue. This provides a clearer understanding of what is happening inside each batch.

Production control: starting birds, mortality, current birds, weights and sales.

Cost control: chick cost, carriage, feed, medicine, electricity and operating expenses.

Historical accuracy: locked COGS keeps previous sale results from changing when new costs are entered.

Stakeholder visibility: separate admin and investor performance reporting.
